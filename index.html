🔍 Analysis

Peak Year: 2008

The industry peaked in 2008 with 538M units sold. This coincides with the late PS2/early PS3/Xbox 360 era, when gaming became mainstream. Sales have declined since due to digital distribution and mobile gaming.

Growth Period: 1995-2008

The industry experienced explosive growth from 1995 to 2008, with the PlayStation revolutionizing 3D gaming and the PS2/Xbox/GC generation expanding the market significantly.

💡 Key Findings

Genre Specialization

• PS2 & PS3: Dominated by Sports and Action games
• Xbox 360: Shooter paradise (Halo, Call of Duty)
• Nintendo consoles: Platform and Fighting games
• PC: Simulation and Strategy specialization
• Handhelds (GBA, DS, 3DS): Platform and RPG focus

Console Identity

Each console developed a unique identity based on its controller and audience. Xbox became the shooter console, Nintendo the family-friendly platformer, and PlayStation the all-rounder.

🌏 Cultural Differences in Gaming

Japanese gamers prefer RPGs, visual novels, and unique IPs like Hot Shots Golf. Western audiences gravitate toward shooters (Call of Duty, Halo) and sports games (FIFA, Madden). This reflects broader cultural preferences for storytelling vs. competitive multiplayer experiences.
